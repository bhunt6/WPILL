About WPILL
What is WPILL?

First established in 1992, WPILL is a journal for undergraduate and graduate students in the GMU English Department who are seeking to bring their work to publication in academic journals.

How does it work?

Through a rigorous peer review process (by your actual peers), WPILL seeks to familiarize students with the academic publication process. Published once a year, the journal is open to all topics related to languge use and utilizes both APA and MLA formats.

Is this something I can list on my CV?

Absolutely! If accepted, your blind peer-reviewed article will be published (digitally) and freely available on the website. You can check out previous issues and articles from the Archive tab in the nav bar.

How long does this process take?

Of course, scholarly journals vary widely in their timelines for review and publication, and many are notorious for their slow pace. Our timeline aims to be much quicker, with submission to publication (if accepted) taking around 3-4 months. Check our submissions page for the current submissions timeline.

If accepted, can I then go on to work on the paper further and submit it somewhere else?

Yes. Working Papers is a common concept in academia, and in fact, Mason has two other Working Paper journals (one in Economics and one in Law). While these journals vary in their intent and makeup, they all share the same purpose: publication of a "working draft" which is preliminary in form and subject to revision before publication in scholarly journals. We understand this can be a puzzling concept to think about at first, and we are happy to discuss further with anyone who might have concerns.

Who are the peer reviewers?

We have recruited current and former students from the Linguistics and Writing & Rhetoric programs to blindly review work for the journal. As a result, our reviewers remain anonymous. Please don't try and figure out who they are!
